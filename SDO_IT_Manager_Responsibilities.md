DEFINING IT AND OT
INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (IT)
IT refers to systems, infrastructure, and applications that support business operations and enterprise processes.
These systems enable planning, administration, and decision-making across the organization.
Examples: Key Characteristics:
• ERP systems (SAP, AS400) • Supports business and administrative processes
• Warehouse Management Systems (WMS) • Runs on standard IT infrastructure
• Corporate networks, servers, databases • Focused on data integrity, availability, and security
• End-user devices (PCs, laptops, mobile) • Does not directly control physical equipment
• Business applications (Finance, HR, CRM)
OPERATIONAL TECHNOLOGY (OT)
OT refers to hardware and software that monitors and controls physical processes in manufacturing, logistics, and
industrial environments. These systems interact with machines, sensors, and production lines in real time.
Examples: Key Characteristics:
• PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers) • Directly interfaces with physical equipment and
• SCADA systems processes
• MES (Manufacturing Execution Systems) • Real-time control and monitoring
• Traceability systems • Prioritizes safety, reliability, and uptime
• Robotics, sensors, actuators • Often requires specialized industrial protocols

IT SITE DELIVERY & OPERATIONS (SDO)
RESPONSIBILITIES
1. Own site IT outcomes
Single point of accountability for IT performance, reliability, and delivery at the site.
2. Run day-to-day IT operations
Own incidents, problems, changes, and continuity. Meet SLAs. Keep the plant running.
3. Own site IT delivery and spend
Accountable for site IT budget, project execution, and delivery of local priorities.
4. Represent site needs in enterprise IT strategy
Translate site goals into clear input for application and infrastructure strategy.
5. Drive transparency and governance
Provide clear visibility of initiatives, service performance, and delivery status.
6. Enable adoption of standard IT capabilities
Ensure global tools and policies are understood, adopted, and used effectively.
7. Lead continuous improvement
Partner with site leaders to improve service quality, efficiency, and resilience.
8. Maintain cyber and technology resilience
Ensure endpoint, hosting, and network environments remain secure and fit for purpose.
